METHODOLOGY

Research Design

The experiment is to glaze clay pots using latex paint — therefore this study is experimental. The researchers will evaluate the characteristics of the clay pots through determining the resistance of the pot and it's brittleness when subjected to different temperature test such as being exposed to sunlight, radiation and heat from oven, and inside the freezer. The exposure to heat from the sun, and the heat and radiation from the oven determines the resistance of the pot while placing it inside the freezer determines the pots brittleness.


Materials and Procedure

First, prepare the following materials and equipment: clay pots, latex paint, a paintbrush, a clean area to work in, and safety equipment such as gloves, an apron, and a mask to ensure safety and cleanliness. After preparing the materials, begin painting the pots with the paintbrush using latex paint (Note: Only half of each pot should be painted, so the other half can be used as a comparison to the modified pots). While painting, ensure that all pots receive the same number of strokes and amount of latex paint, so the experiment remains consistent across all pots. Also, make sure to paint both the inside and outside of the clay pots. After painting, place the pots in a clean and safe area and allow them to dry before starting the experimentation.


Experimental Pots

A total of 30 clay pots will be used in the experiment, with half of the pots glazed with latex paint, and the other half used as a comparison. To create a pot glazed with latex paint, first apply the latex paint to both the inside and outside of the clay pots, then wait for them to dry before the experimentation begins.


Experimental design

There are a total of 30 clay pots, divided into 3 groups, each containing 5 clay pots glazed with latex paint and 5 clay pots that are not modified. There will be 3 tests: exposing the pots to the sun, placing them inside an oven, and placing them inside a freezer. The diameter, height, shape, quality, and thickness of the pots will be consistent across all samples to ensure there is no bias in the experiment. The quality of the clay pots glazed with latex paint is the dependent variable, while the independent variables are the amount of latex paint, the number of strokes, and the constant variables include the height, diameter, thickness, quality of the pot, and the different experiments. In the solar radiation test, the clay pots will be directly exposed to the sun from 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM for 7 days to observe any effects of sunlight on the pots. In the oven test, the pots will be subjected to 15-minute intervals at increasing temperatures of 50°C, 75°C, and 100°C, with 1 hour between each trial. For the freezer test, the pots will be placed inside a freezer at -212°F (-100°C) for 8 hours. After each day of experimentation, observe the pots for cracks, breakage, or any deformities, and record the observations. Then, compare the results of the different pots based on the amount of damage done in each test to determine which pot performs the best.


Statistical Test Analysis

The researchers will utilize a one tailed t-test, hypothesis testing using ANOVA or Analysis of variance.
The researchers will utilize a t-test since the population and sample size are not significant, with the sample population being less than 12 for each experiment. The study will use a onetailed test to determine if the null hypothesis will be rejected, as the scope is on whether the variables are directly connected. In this case, only the positive critical region will be considered, as the negative critical region relates to the possibility of an inverse correlation, which is irrelevant to the study. The study will also use ANOVA to analyze whether the differences between sample groups, which vary in the amount of latex paint used, are statistically significant.
