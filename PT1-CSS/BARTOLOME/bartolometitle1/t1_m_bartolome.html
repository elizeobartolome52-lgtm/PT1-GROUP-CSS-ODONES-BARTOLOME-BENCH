METHODOLOGY

  1. The Researchers will conduct a review of the existing literature and research on organic watercolor. This will provide a foundation of knowledge and help for further investigation.

  2. Next Researchers will study the properties and investigate the environmental impact of organic watercolor.

  3. Then the Researchers will develop an experimental design that includes the selection of organic pigments and other materials. They will determine the variables to be tested for comparison.

  4. Now the Researchers will conduct experiments to test the selected organic pigments by observing and recording the color intensity, transparency, texture and other relevant properties of the watercolor paints.

  5. After the experimentation, the Researchers will now collect and record the data obtained from the experiments.

  6. After collecting data, the Researchers will now compare the results of the different organic pigments to the characteristics of the manufactured watercolor like texture, intensity of color, and pigment.

  7. The Researchers also evaluating the organic watercolors and manufactured watercolor in terms of their performance, quality, and suitability for watercolor painting.

  8. Then, the Researchers will document the findings of the research in a clear and organized manner by making charts, graphs, and visual representations of the experimental results.

  9. Afterwards, The Researchers will now provide recommendations for further research or improvements in organic watercolor materials, techniques and suggest potential avenues for future studies.
